Alpine lodge immersion

Four nights at The Lindis with guided riding, private stargazing, and a private helicopter/glacier adventure to Aoraki/Mount Cook and the Tasman Glacier.

Three nights near Arthur's Pass with a station tour, guided horse trek, all meals, and time for hikes, bouldering, spa, fishing, biking, or Castle Hill.

Three nights at Huka Lodge with a privately guided Waimangu Volcanic Valley walk, then one waterfront night at Park Hyatt Auckland.

Day-By-Day Itinerary

Planned travel, included experiences, drive times, and optional ideas.

Sunday Sep 27

Arrive Auckland, fly to Queenstown

Southern Crossings meets you airside at Auckland International Airport, assists with customs and luggage, and transfers you by private vehicle to the domestic terminal for the Auckland to Queenstown flight.

On arrival in Queenstown, collect a full-size automatic SUV rental vehicle with daily insurance, unlimited kilometres, and loss damage waiver. Drive about 20 minutes to ROKI Collection Queenstown.

Auckland to Queenstown flight SUV rental begins ROKI - 4 nights
Monday Sep 28

Milford Sound by fixed-wing flight and cruise

Transfer from ROKI to Queenstown Airport for a shared fixed-wing scenic flight to Milford Sound, with views of hanging valleys, alpine lakes, and mountain summits. The flight is about 35-40 minutes each way.

After landing, join a shared guided nature cruise for 1 hour 45 minutes. The cruise includes morning tea and the chance to see seals, birds, rare dolphins, penguins, waterfalls, and the sheer fiord walls before flying back to Queenstown.

Shared excursion Approx. 4 hr 30 min total Morning tea on board
Tuesday Sep 29

Glenorchy wilderness jet boat

Drive independently to Glenorchy, about 45 minutes from Queenstown, for a shared wilderness jet boat excursion into the braided glacier-fed rivers near Mt Aspiring National Park.

The activity includes briefing/check-in and about two hours on the water, with guides covering local history, the Greenstone Trail, and Maori legends. If conditions allow, there may be a brief walk, with time for lunch in Glenorchy.

Approx. 2 hr 30 min activity Lunch in Glenorchy 45 min drive each way
Wednesday Sep 30

Queenstown at leisure

A free day in Queenstown with optional local experiences suggested by Southern Crossings.

Optional Queenstown ideas
  • Visit historic Arrowtown and its heritage-building boutiques.
  • Golf at Jack's Point, The Hills, or Millbrook.
  • Bungy jump at Nevis River, Kawarau Bridge, or The Ledge.
  • Walk or hike lakeside, rainforest, high-country, alpine, or farmland trails.
  • Visit film sites used for The Lord of the Rings, The Hobbit, and other productions.
  • Choose high-adrenaline activities such as skydiving, canyoning, or quad biking.
  • Cruise Lake Wakatipu on the TSS Earnslaw to Walter Peak High Country Station.
  • Ride the Skyline Gondola to Bob's Peak and add the luge if you want extra altitude fun.
Thursday Oct 1

Drive Queenstown to the Ahuriri Valley

Drive north from Queenstown to the Ahuriri Valley, about 2 hours 30 minutes. Suggested stops and detours include Arrowtown, the Crown Range Road, Wanaka, and the Lindis Pass.

Check in to The Lindis for four nights in a King Master Suite, including pre-dinner drinks and all meals.

Approx. 2 hr 30 min drive The Lindis - 4 nights All meals included
Friday Oct 2

The Lindis horse trek and private stargazing

Meet your guide at the stables for an intermediate guided trek through high-country farmland, native beech forest, and subalpine herb fields. The ride is about two hours.

That evening, a private guide takes you to a secluded valley location for stargazing. The session covers bright stars, star clusters, Greek and Maori constellations, celestial navigation, deep-sky objects, and planets through a telescope.

2 hr guided trek Private stargazing 1 hr 30 min - 2 hr night session
Saturday Oct 3

Private helicopter adventure to Aoraki/Mount Cook

Depart The Lindis by private helicopter across the Southern Alps toward Aoraki/Mount Cook, with close views of snow-covered summits, Tasman Glacier, blue ice formations, seracs, crevasses, and the Hochstetter Icefall.

Land on the glacier for a guided hike with crampons and an expert guide. The day includes a picnic lunch in a scenic remote location and a return flight over the Mackenzie Region.

Private helicopter Tasman Glacier hike Picnic lunch included
Sunday Oct 4

The Lindis at leisure

A flexible lodge day in the Ahuriri Valley, with optional experiences subject to availability and best reserved in advance.

Optional Ahuriri Valley ideas
  • Fly fishing near the lodge, subject to seasonality.
  • Mountain e-biking through rugged countryside.
  • Guided or independent hiking through wetlands, forests, tussocks, and alpine terrain.
  • 4WD buggy exploration of the property.
  • Self-guided ecology tour focused on local flora and fauna.
  • Gliding over the Waitaki Valley.
  • Guided clay-target shooting and archery.
  • Guided Ahuriri Valley tour along Birchwood Road, with wetlands, wildlife, and geological context.
  • Private BBQ outdoors or at Black Diamond Hut.
Monday Oct 5

Drive Ahuriri Valley to Arthur's Pass

Continue north through the South Island from Ahuriri Valley to Arthur's Pass, about five hours of driving. The route passes through Mackenzie Country, with an early-start option to detour to Aoraki/Mount Cook before crossing the Canterbury Plains toward the Southern Alps.

Southern Crossings suggests visiting the limestone formations at Castle Hill en route. Check in to Flockhill Lodge for three nights in a King One Bedroom Deluxe Villa, including farm tour and all meals.

Approx. 5 hr drive Flockhill Lodge - 3 nights All meals included
Tuesday Oct 6

Flockhill station tour and lodge time

Take an interactive high-country station tour at Flockhill. The tour heads into the back blocks and explains station history, farming life, Merino-Romney sheep work, and the property's biodiversity and regenerative farming plans.

Approx. 1 hr 30 min tour Working high-country station
Optional Flockhill ideas
  • Hike to caves, waterfalls, and mountain lookouts.
  • Go caving in the Southern Alps.
  • Visit Castle Hill, a revered geological formation.
  • Try guided bouldering.
  • Mountain bike or e-bike through Craigieburn Forest.
  • Stargaze under clear high-country skies.
  • Fly fish on remote mountain rivers.
  • Kayak or stand-up paddleboard on Lake Moana Rua.
  • Use the onsite wellness spa, gym, yoga room, treatment rooms, sauna, hot tub, and cold plunge.
  • Golf at Terrace Downs High Country Championship Course.
Wednesday Oct 7

Guided Lake Coleridge horse trek

Journey to Lake Coleridge for a guided horseback experience through mountain and farmland landscapes. The itinerary notes traditional Western or Australian stock saddles and a high-country riding feel.

Approx. 7 hr total Includes approx. 2 hr driving Guided horse trek
Thursday Oct 8

Drive to Christchurch, fly to Rotorua, drive to Taupo

Drive through the Southern Alps from Arthur's Pass to Christchurch Airport, about 1 hour 30 minutes, and return the rental vehicle before the Christchurch to Rotorua flight.

  • 10:10am suggested check-in.
  • 11:10am depart Christchurch on Air New Zealand flight NZ5782.
  • 1:00pm arrive Rotorua.

Collect another full-size automatic SUV rental vehicle and drive about one hour to Huka Lodge in Taupo. Check in for three nights in a King Lodge Suite with Fireplace, including pre-dinner drinks, dinner, and breakfast.

Christchurch to Rotorua flight Second SUV rental begins Huka Lodge - 3 nights
Friday Oct 9

Privately guided Waimangu Volcanic Valley walk

Drive independently to Waimangu Volcanic Valley, about 45 minutes from Huka Lodge, for a privately guided geothermal walk.

The walk includes Frying Pan Lake, Inferno Crater, steaming hills, newly forming silica terraces, rare thermal plants, and the broader Rotorua geothermal landscape.

Approx. 45 min drive Private guided walk Approx. 2 hr 30 min duration
Saturday Oct 10

Huka Lodge at leisure

A flexible day around Taupo, the Waikato River, Lake Taupo, geothermal country, and Huka Lodge.

Optional Taupo and Huka ideas
  • Scenic Lake Taupo cruise, Maori rock carvings, paddleboarding, or fishing.
  • Fly fishing on rivers or trolling on Lake Taupo.
  • Visit Huka Falls and Waikato River trails.
  • Golf at Kinloch or Wairakei.
  • Visit culturally significant tribal Maori locations with a local guide.
  • Ride the Huka Falls Jet.
  • Walk Aratiatia Scenic Reserve and time the dam-gate release.
  • Explore geothermal areas such as Craters of the Moon, Wai-O-Tapu, or Rotorua.
  • Hike the Tongariro Crossing.
  • Add adventure options such as bungy jumping, quad biking, skydiving, or gliding.
  • Walk or mountain bike through local bush.
  • Visit Lava Glass and its sculpture garden.

Sunday Oct 11

Drive Taupo to Auckland

Drive north from Taupo to Auckland, about four hours, through the Waikato's rolling farmland. Southern Crossings suggests optional stops at Hobbiton or the Waitomo glowworm caves before arriving in the city.

Check in to Park Hyatt Auckland for one night in a King Harbour View Room with Balcony, including breakfast.

Approx. 4 hr drive Optional Hobbiton or Waitomo Park Hyatt - 1 night
Monday Oct 12

Depart Auckland

Drive to Auckland International Airport, about 45 minutes, return the rental vehicle, and depart New Zealand.

Approx. 45 min airport drive International departure

Property Notes

Key lodge and hotel details from the attached itinerary.

ROKI Collection Queenstown

Waterfront Queenstown property with views across Lake Wakatipu to The Remarkables. The itinerary highlights 15 suites designed by Virginia Fisher, fireplaces, indulgent bathtubs, and separate walk-in wardrobes.

The Lindis Lodge

Contemporary luxury lodge in the Ahuriri Valley on a 6,000-acre high-country property. The design sits low in the landscape, with floor-to-ceiling views, Master Suites, Lodge Suites, Pods, dining in the Great Hall, bar, lounge spaces, games room, and billiards room.

Flockhill Lodge

Historic high-country station near Arthur's Pass, established in 1857 and spread over about 36,000 acres of pastures, basins, forests, streams, rivers, tussock, boulder fields, and mountains.

Huka Lodge

Iconic lodge founded in the 1920s on 17 manicured acres beside the Waikato River, upstream from Huka Falls. The itinerary emphasizes river views, highly considered service, suites, cottages, and two exclusive private hideaways.

Park Hyatt Auckland

Waterfront hotel on Wynyard Quarter's Lighter Basin, with Waitemata Harbour views, 195 rooms, four restaurants and bars, event spaces, a day spa, 25-metre infinity pool, and fitness centre.

Season Notes

Planning context from Southern Crossings.

Late September and early October

Southern Crossings describes this as a lovely spring travel window: fresh green landscapes, lengthening days, crisp alpine scenery, comfortable daytime temperatures, and fewer crowds than peak summer. South Island alpine weather can still be changeable, while the North Island is generally becoming milder and more settled.
